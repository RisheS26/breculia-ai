Step-by-step: discovery call to live outbound

1

15-min discovery call

We talk through your ICP (ideal customer profile): target industry, company size range, decision-maker title, geography, and what a good reply looks like for your business. You share editor access to your Clay account and campaign access to your Instantly account. We learn the context that separates your best-fit prospects from wasted credits.

You leave with a clear picture of what the system will look like before a single dollar changes hands.

Day 0 — 15 minutes
2

Clay enrichment waterfall built

We build your Clay table from scratch on your account. The enrichment waterfall pulls from Apollo as primary, with Hunter and Prospeo as fallbacks — so you get verified emails even when Apollo misses. Each row is enriched for first name, work email, job title, company, website, funding stage, employee count, and LinkedIn URL.

The waterfall is structured to maximize enrichment coverage while minimizing Clay credit burn — the exact logic most DIY tables miss.

Day 1 — in progress
3

AI openers written, Instantly campaign configured

For each of the 500 initial leads, the AI reads the company signals from Clay — what they do, their funding stage, their recent activity — and writes a unique first line tailored to that specific prospect. Not a mail-merge token. A real observation about their business that makes them want to keep reading.

Your Instantly campaign is configured with warmed sending domains, optimized send timing, sequence logic, and deliverability settings (DKIM, SPF, DMARC verified). The campaign is reviewed end-to-end before first send.

Day 1–2 — build complete
4

Campaign goes live. Loom walkthrough delivered.

Your first 200 personalized emails start sending. A 20-minute Loom walkthrough is delivered to your email — showing exactly what was built in Clay, how the enrichment waterfall works, what the Instantly campaign structure looks like, and how to read the metrics that matter.

You're not handed a black box. You understand your own system.

Why the system works

Three reasons generic cold email fails — and what the system does differently.

Enrichment waterfall, not single-source

Most DIY Clay tables pull from one data source. When it misses — and it does, often — the row is wasted. The waterfall structure ensures each lead gets enriched by a secondary source (Hunter) and then a tertiary (Prospeo) before giving up. Result: higher valid email percentage, fewer bounces, better sender reputation.

AI-personalized openers, not mail-merge

The opening line of every email is written by an AI that has read that specific company's data from Clay — funding stage, what they actually do, growth signals. The result is a first line that references something real about their business, not a token like "Hi {{firstName}}, I loved your recent blog post." Prospects can tell the difference.

Deliverability built-in from day one

Domains are warmed before first send. DKIM, SPF, and DMARC are verified. Sending volume is ramped gradually. Send timing is optimized to the recipient's time zone. Domain health is monitored weekly. Most DIY Instantly setups skip half of this — then wonder why they land in spam at month two.
